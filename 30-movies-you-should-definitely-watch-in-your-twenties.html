30 Movies You Should Definitely Watch In Your Twenties

July 2024 · 20 minute read

Your 20s are a time to try new things, grow, and learn about yourself. It’s a time when you’re trying to figure out who you are and what you want to do with your life. And what better way to get through this important decade than by watching some great movies?

Movies can take us to new places, teach us important lessons, and motivate us to be our best selves. You’ll probably have a wide range of experiences and feelings in your twenties, and these movies can help you make sense of it all.

But what is it about a movie that makes it perfect for your twenties? So, it’s all about being real. In this decade, the movies that speak to us most are the ones that reflect our own lives, problems, and hopes. They show what it’s like to be young and unsure, and they also make us want to dream big and follow our passions.

Whether you’re trying to figure out love and relationships, your place in the world, or just who you are, these movies can help. They give us comfort, wisdom, and ideas, and they remind us that we are not alone on our journey.

No one’s twenties are exactly the same, so this list of movies is by no means complete. But it’s a place to start, a collection of movies that a lot of twentysomethings have liked over the years. Some are old favourites, while others are newer, but they all have one thing in common: they capture the spirit of youth and the beauty of not knowing.

So, if you want a story that will make you cry, make you laugh out loud, or make you feel good about growing up, this list has you covered. These movies will change the way you think about love, friendship, family, work, and everything else. They’ll make you want to dream big and follow your passions, and they’ll also remind you that it’s okay to stumble and make mistakes along the way.

So get comfortable and let these movies show you how to live through one of the most exciting and life-changing decades of your life.

20th Century Women (2016)

20th Century Women is a beautiful and touching movie about motherhood, friendship, and growing up. It takes place in the late 1970s. The movie is about Dorothea, a single mother who asks two younger women to help her raise her son, Jamie, who is a teenager. The movie looks at how the changing times and gender roles affect the relationships and identities of the people in it.

The characters are deep and well-rounded, and there are many beautiful and memorable scenes in the movie. The movie 20th Century Women will make you laugh, cry, and feel every emotion in between. It shows how important relationships are and how important it is to take risks and accept change.

Amélie (2001)

Amélie is a silly romantic comedy that takes place in Paris. Amélie is a shy and creative young woman who decides to help the people around her. This is the story of the movie. Along the way, she falls in love with Nino, a young man with a similar sense of humour to hers. The movie is a celebration of life’s small pleasures, and Amélie’s charm makes it impossible not to fall in love with her. The movie is beautiful to look at. It has bright colours and a fun soundtrack. This movie is based on the idea that life is full of magic and wonder, and that sometimes all it takes to make the world a better place is a little bit of imagination.

Before Sunrise (1995)

Before Sunrise is a love story that takes place in Vienna over one night. The movie is about an American traveller named Jesse and a French student named Celine. They meet on a train and decide to spend the night in Vienna together. They walk around the city and talk about life, love, and everything in between. Over the course of the night, they get to know each other very well. The movie is beautifully shot, and Ethan Hawke and Julie Delpy have electric chemistry on screen. This movie shows that sometimes the most meaningful connections can happen in the most unexpected places, and that true love is about more than just physical attraction.

Begin Again (2013)

Begin Again is a touching movie about two people who have lost their way and find each other through music. Gretta, a writer who just got dumped, meets Dan, a music executive who used to love the business but no longer does. They go on a musical journey together that helps them discover themselves and each other. The movie shows that sometimes the people and things you least expect can bring you the most joy and happiness in life.

Black Swan (2010)

If you want a movie that is both a psychological thriller and a beautiful piece of art to look at, Black Swan is the one for you. Natalie Portman plays a ballerina who gets the lead role in “Swan Lake.” As she plays the Black Swan more and more, she starts to lose touch with reality. The movie looks at things like trying to be perfect, mental health, and trying to be the best no matter what.

Black Swan is a movie that will stay with you long after the credits have rolled. It has amazing dance scenes, haunting images, and a powerful performance by Natalie Portman. It’s a reminder that trying to be the best can be expensive and that the line between reality and delusion can be dangerously thin at times.

Bo Burnham: Inside (2021)

During the COVID-19 pandemic, comedian Bo Burnham wrote, directed, and performed a one-man show called Bo Burnham: Inside. The movie is a musical comedy about being alone, being creative, and feeling lonely. The songs are funny and sad at the same time, and the visual effects are beautiful. The plot is a reflection on what it was like to be a performer when live shows were impossible and how being alone can hurt our mental health. Most of the movie is about how art can bring comfort, connection, and hope during hard times. It’s a funny and touching look at what people went through during the pandemic.

Columbus (2017)

Columbus is a beautiful and moving movie that looks at how architecture and the human experience are connected. The movie is about a young woman who stays in Columbus, Indiana to take care of her mother and a Korean-born man who comes to the city to take care of his estranged architect father. The movie is thoughtful and enjoyable because it moves slowly and pays attention to every detail. The movie’s look at grief, regret, and forgiveness will make you see the power of architecture and the human spirit in a new way.

Dear Zindagi (2016)

Dear Zindagi is a drama about coming of age that looks at how mental health and relationships can be complicated. The movie is about Kaira, a young filmmaker who is trying to figure out what she wants to do with her life. She goes to see a therapist, who is played by Shah Rukh Khan. The therapist helps her deal with her past traumas and understand the problems she is having now. The movie shows in a beautiful way how important it is to take care of yourself and ask for help when you need it. It also looks at how family relationships are complicated and how they can affect our mental health. The characters are interesting and easy to relate to, and the movie is full of heartwarming and thought-provoking scenes.

Eternal Sunshine Of The Spotless Mind (2004)

Have you ever wished you could forget about someone? In the movie Eternal Sunshine of the Spotless Mind, this is exactly what happens. Jim Carrey and Kate Winslet play two people who used to be together but broke up. After a painful breakup, they decide to have a procedure to erase each other from their memories. But as they relive their relationship backwards, they realise that they don’t want to forget each other after all.

This movie is a moving look at love, memory, and what it means to be human. It shows that even if we try to forget the people and things that hurt us, they still shape who we are and who we are with. Eternal Sunshine of the Spotless Mind is a must-see for anyone dealing with love and loss. It has beautiful visuals, a unique way of telling the story, and great acting.

Frances Ha (2012)

The movie Frances Ha is funny and charming. It’s about a 27-year-old woman named Frances who wants to be a dancer and is trying to make it in New York City. The movie shows her life as she makes decisions about friendships, relationships, and jobs. This movie stands out because it is easy to understand. You can’t help but root for Frances, and her struggles with becoming an adult and finding her place in the world are relatable to everyone. The main message of the movie is that things don’t always go as planned, and that’s okay. It’s important to enjoy the journey and be open to what comes your way.

In The Mood For Love (2000)

In the 1960s, Hong Kong is the setting for the romantic drama In the Mood for Love. The movie is beautiful to look at. In the movie, Mr. Chow and Mrs. Chan, who live next door to each other, find out that their husbands and wives are having an affair with each other. As they spend more time together, they start to like each other, but their sense of duty and proper behaviour keeps them from acting on their feelings. The movie makes you think about love, loss, and chances you didn’t take. The characters are shown in a beautiful way, and the filming is stunning. The lesson we can learn from this movie is that we shouldn’t give up on our dreams just because we can’t always go after what we want.

Lady Bird (2017)

Lady Bird is a funny, charming, and relatable coming-of-age story about a teenage girl who deals with the ups and downs of high school, college applications, and the complicated relationships she has with her mother, friends, and potential love interests. With a nuanced script and great acting from the cast, the movie shows all of the angst, drama, and insecurities that come with being a teenager. Lady Bird is a movie that everyone in their twenties should watch at least once. It’s about identity, acceptance, and finding yourself.

Licorice Pizza (2021)

In this coming-of-age story set in California in the 1970s, a 15-year-old boy named Gary wants to be an actor, and a 25-year-old woman named Alana wants to be a photographer. As they try to find their way in the world of Hollywood and teen romance, they learn important lessons about friendship, ambition, and growing up. The movie is full of music and references that make you feel old, and the chemistry between the two main characters is clear. Licorice Pizza is a funny, heartwarming movie that will make you laugh, cry, and want to dance.

Marriage Story (2019)

Marriage Story is a powerful drama that looks at how love, relationships, and divorce are all complicated. The movie is about a couple named Charlie and Nicole who are getting a messy and sad divorce. As they work through the legal and emotional parts of getting a divorce, they struggle to understand how they feel about each other and how their separation is affecting their young son. The movie is real, sad, and heartbreaking, but it also shows how strong the human spirit can be when it’s put to the test. Marriage Story is a movie that will show you how important it is to talk to people, forgive them, and care about them in all of your relationships.

Mistress America (2015)

Mistress America is a great choice if you want a funny, weird, and relatable comedy. Noah Baumbach is in charge of the movie, which is about a college freshman named Tracy who is trying to find her place in the world. She meets her soon-to-be stepsister Brooke, who is played by Greta Gerwig and is everything she is not: confident, outgoing, and full of life.

The movie shows how complicated female friendships, ambition, and figuring out who you are can be. Brooke is trying to make her dreams come true, while Tracy is trying to find her voice. People can relate to and like the characters, and the movie is full of funny one-liners and scenes that will stick with you. Mistress America is a refreshing and entertaining movie that will make you feel inspired and uplifted.

Never Rarely Sometimes Always (2020)

In Never Rarely Sometimes Always, a teenage girl goes to New York City to get an abortion, which is a very sad story. The story is powerful for how simple it is. It’s about a girl and her cousin who are going on a trip. The story shows how they deal with the practical and emotional challenges of the trip. The movie makes a strong case against the problems women face when trying to get reproductive health care and the trauma that can come from these problems.

The movie never feels preachy or heavy-handed, and the characters have depth and are easy to relate to. The movie makes us really appreciate the bravery and strength of women who have to fight for their right to control their bodies. It’s a reminder of how important it is to support access to health care and the right to have children.

On The Beach At Night Alone (2017)

In On the Beach at Night Alone, a woman’s search for love, acceptance, and self-worth is shown in a touching and personal way. The movie tells the story of a Korean actress who has an affair with a married man and has to deal with the consequences. The movie is hauntingly beautiful because of how real the emotions are, how beautiful the cinematography is, and how well the actors play their parts. The movie explores themes of loneliness, heartbreak, and self-discovery in a way that is both gentle and powerful. It will make you see the beauty of life in a new way. 10

Parasite (2019)

The movie Parasite is dark, weird, and makes you think about things like class, wealth, and privilege. The movie tells the story of the Kim family, who live in a small South Korean apartment in the basement and struggle to make ends meet. But when Ki-woo gets a job teaching a rich family’s daughter, the Kims see a chance to get out of poverty.

What happens next is a series of tense and scary events that will make you wonder about society and the human condition. The movie Parasite will stay with you long after the credits roll, and it will make you question what you think about privilege, inequality, and how far people will go to stay alive.

Portrait Of A Lady On Fire (2019)

Portrait of a Lady on Fire is a beautiful and emotionally powerful movie about love, passion, and the way women look at each other. The movie is about a painter named Marianne, who is asked to paint a portrait of a young woman named Hélose, who is about to get married to a stranger. Marianne is drawn to Hélose in ways she never thought possible as she spends more time with her. What comes next is a beautiful and haunting look at how complicated female desires are and how power works between men and women. Portrait of a Lady on Fire is a movie that will make you realise how important it is to live your life to the fullest and be amazed by the beauty and complexity of human emotions.

Reality Bites (1994)


Reality Bites is a classic coming-of-age movie from the 1990s. It shows how a group of friends deal with life after college. The movie is about Lelaina, who just got out of film school and is having trouble finding her place in the world. She and her friends Troy, Vickie, and Sammy are all trying to figure out how to deal with the stresses of being adults.

The movie touches on career, relationships, and who we are as people. You can relate to and like the characters, and you can’t help but cheer for them as they go through the ups and downs of their twenties. From this movie, you can learn that life doesn’t always go as planned, and that’s okay. Sometimes, you have to go through a time of not knowing what to do and doubting yourself before you can find your way.

Shiva Baby (2020)

Shiva Baby is a dark comedy about a young woman who goes to a shiva with her parents and finds out that her sugar daddy is also there. The plot is both funny and cringe-worthy because the main character has to deal with her own problems while trying to deal with the awkwardness of the situation. All of the characters, from the overbearing parents to the ex-girlfriend who is also there, are bigger than life.

The movie is a comment on the pressures that young people face in their careers, relationships, and family expectations. Overall, Shiva Baby is a reminder that we’re all just trying to figure things out and that it’s okay to stumble and make mistakes along the way. It’s a funny and relatable look at how messy life can be.

Short Term 12 (2013)

Short Term 12 is a powerful movie about Grace, a supervisor at a group home for troubled teens, who is struggling with her own past trauma. The movie looks at mental health, trauma, and resilience. It also shows how empathy and kindness can have a huge effect on other people. In this movie, the characters are complicated and have many sides, and their struggles feel very real. Everyone has a story, and sometimes all they need is someone to listen and understand. It’s a reminder to be kind to others and to ourselves, and to always look for the light in the dark.

Take This Waltz (2011)

Take This Waltz is about a writer named Margot. She is happily married, but all of a sudden she feels a strong attraction to her neighbour, Daniel. As Margot tries to figure out how she feels, the movie shows how difficult relationships, commitment, and desire can be. The main lesson of the movie is that things aren’t always better on the other side. Instead of always looking for something new, it’s important to value what you already have and improve it.

The Devil Wears Prada (2006)

The Devil Wears Prada is a great movie for people who are just starting to work and feel a little lost. Meryl Streep plays Miranda Priestly, a tough and demanding fashion editor. Anne Hathaway plays a want tobe journalist who gets a job as her assistant. As she deals with the high-stakes world of fashion and the stresses of her job and personal life, she learns important lessons about persistence, hard work, and how important it is to be true to herself. The Devil Wears Prada is a great movie to watch when you need to be inspired and reminded that you can do anything you set your mind to. It has a great cast, memorable fashion moments, and funny dialogue.

The Father (2020)

Anthony Hopkins gives a hauntingly brilliant performance in The Father as an old man who is losing his memories. The movie shows how his character loses himself as he tries to deal with his worsening mental health and keep his sense of self. The story doesn’t go in a straight line, which adds to the feeling of being lost and confused. Olivia Colman plays his daughter, who is trying to care for and help him but also has her own problems to deal with.

The movie is a strong reminder of how fragile the mind is and how ageing can hurt our bodies and minds. The main thing to take away is a deep sense of compassion for people with memory loss and the people who care for them. It shows how complicated the relationship between parents and children can be as they face the challenges of getting older.

The Florida Project (2017)

The Florida Project is a heartwarming and heartbreaking coming-of-age story about a smart 6-year-old girl and her single mother who get into trouble near Disney World. The movie shows how colourful and real the lives of poor people in a state where dreams and magic are said to happen are. The child actor’s amazing performance and the comments on the harsh truths of life will make you question the idea of the American dream.

The Lobster (2015)

Are you sick of all the stress that comes with dating and looking for love? Then you should watch The Lobster. In this strange black comedy, single people have 45 days to find a partner or they will turn into an animal of their choice. The movie shows David, who is played by Colin Farrell, making his way through this strange world and its strange rules.

The movie The Lobster stands out because of how strange and funny it is. It takes a satirical look at how people are often forced to live up to society’s expectations about love and relationships. The characters are strange and complicated, and the movie makes you think a lot about love and being yourself.

The Social Network (2010)

Facebook has changed the way we talk and connect with each other, whether we like it or not. The Social Network is an exciting drama about how Facebook came to be and about the people who made it happen. Mark Zuckerberg, a smart but awkward programmer, is played by Jesse Eisenberg. His best friend and co-founder, Eduardo Saverin, is played by Andrew Garfield. Saverin is pushed out of the company.

The movie looks at friendship, ambition, and betrayal, and it also asks important questions about how technology is changing our society. The Social Network is a must-see for anyone interested in how technology, business, and culture interact. The story moves quickly, the actors are great, and the music is great.

The Worst Person In The World (2021)

This Norwegian movie is about a young woman named Julie and her relationships, career, and personal growth over the course of several years. As Julie goes through the different stages of her life, she finds it hard to balance her wants and goals with what society expects of her and the pressures of being an adult. The Worst Person In The World is a beautifully shot and emotionally powerful movie that looks at how complicated modern life is and how hard it can be to find yourself.

Unicorn Store (2017)

Do you feel like you have no idea what you want to do with your life? Then you should watch the movie Unicorn Store. This quirky comedy-drama tells the story of Kit, an artist who can’t make it on her own and has to move back in with her parents. But Kit’s life takes a magical turn when she gets an invite to a mysterious store that sells unicorns. Along the way, she learns how important it is to stay true to who you are, follow your dreams, and find your place in the world. The movie is strange, funny, and touching, and it will make you want to follow your own dreams, no matter how strange they may be.
