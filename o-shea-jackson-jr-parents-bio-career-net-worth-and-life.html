Oshea Jackson Jr. Parents, Bio, Career, Net Worth and Life

August 2024 · 3 minute read

O’Shea Jackson Jr. didn’t just stay in his father Ice Cube’s shadow—he made his own mark in showbiz.

He’s not just Ice Cube’s son; he’s a skilled actor, rapper, and songwriter in his own right.

This article looks into O’Shea Jackson Jr.’s life and career, checking out his connections with family and the varied journey that brought him success.

Who is O’shea Jackson Jr. ?

In 1991, O’Shea Jr. was born in Los Angeles, where he saw his father’s quick rise in the music industry.

Ice Cube, a key member of the legendary N.W.A., was already a rap icon with bold lyrics and outspoken social commentary.

Growing up in this environment, music and art became a big part of O’Shea Jr.’s life. Living up to such a powerful legacy could have been tough, but O’Shea Jr. drew inspiration from his parents’ hard work.

His mother, Kimberly Woodruff, who values privacy, gave him strong support and nurtured his creative side.

This close-knit family taught O’Shea Jr. the importance of resilience, family, and using his voice to speak his truth.

O’shea Jackson Jr. Parents

O’Shea Jackson Jr.’s parents are O’Shea Jackson Sr., also known as Ice Cube, and Kimberly Woodruff.

Jackson Jr.’s father, Ice Cube

Ice Cube, a legendary rapper, actor, and filmmaker, gained fame in the late 1980s as part of the influential hip-hop group N.W.A.

They were known for controversial lyrics addressing social and political issues affecting young black people in America.

Ice Cube later pursued a successful solo career, releasing hit albums and starring in popular films.

O’Shea Jackson Jr.’s mother, Kimberly Woodruff

Kimberly Woodruff, a private person, has mostly stayed out of the spotlight. She and Ice Cube were in a relationship for several years before their son, O’Shea Jr. was born in 1991.

Despite no longer being together, they maintain a friendly relationship and co-parent their children. O’Shea Jackson Jr. openly talks about how his parents influenced his life and career.

He credits his father’s work ethic and commitment to social justice as inspirations, while his mother has consistently been his biggest supporter.

In 2015, O’Shea Jr. had a unique opportunity to portray his father in the biopic Straight Outta Compton, which depicted the rise of N.W.A.

Career: From Rap Roots to Hollywood Heights

O’Shea Jr. first stepped into the spotlight through music. Using the stage name “OMG,” he released mixtapes and collaborated with notable artists like Kendrick Lamar and ScHoolboy Q.

His rap style, combining his father’s lyrical skill with a contemporary flow, highlighted his unique voice and talent.

However, O’Shea Jr.’s aspirations went beyond music. In 2015, he secured a pivotal role: portraying his father in the biopic “Straight Outta Compton.”

This wasn’t just any acting job; it was an opportunity to walk in his father’s shoes, understand his experiences, and share them with the world.

His performance received critical acclaim, earning him an NAACP Image Award and thrusting him into the Hollywood spotlight. After this breakthrough, O’Shea Jr. took on diverse roles across genres.

He demonstrated comedic timing in “Ingrid Goes West,” showcased dramatic depth in “Den of Thieves,” and displayed action skills in “Godzilla: King of the Monsters.”

He also returned to his musical roots, contributing original songs to several film soundtracks.
