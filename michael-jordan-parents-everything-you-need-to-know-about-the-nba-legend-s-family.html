Michael Jordan Parents: Everything You Need to Know About the NBA Legends Family

October 2024 · 5 minute read

Michael Jordan is widely regarded as the greatest basketball player of all time, but he is also a family man who has been married twice and has five children.

In this article, we will explore everything you need to know about Michael Jordan’s parents, who played a significant role in his life and career, as well as his spouses, kids, and other relatives.

Michael Jordan Age

Michael Jordan was born on February 17, 1963, in Brooklyn, New York.

His age is currently 60 years old as of September 2023.

He grew up in Wilmington, North Carolina, where he attended Laney High School and developed his passion for basketball.

He later enrolled at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, where he won the national championship in 1982 and became a college star.

Michael Jordan Parents

Michael Jordan’s parents are James R. Jordan Sr. and Deloris Peoples Jordan.

They met in high school in Wallace, North Carolina, and got married in 1956. They had five children: James Jr., Deloris, Larry, Michael, and Roslyn.

James R. Jordan Sr. was a lifelong basketball fan who encouraged and supported his son Michael’s athletic pursuits.

He also taught him valuable lessons about hard work, discipline, and integrity.

He was tragically murdered on July 23, 1993, by two robbers who shot him while he was sleeping in his car at a rest stop in North Carolina.

Deloris Peoples Jordan was also a devoted mother who raised her children with love and care. She instilled in them the importance of education, faith, and perseverance.

Michael Jordan Spouse

Michael Jordan has been married twice in his life.

His first wife was Juanita Vanoy, whom he met in 1984 at a restaurant in Chicago.

They dated for several years and got engaged in 1987.

They filed for divorce twice: once in 2002 and again in 2006.

Their divorce was finalized in December 2006 and was one of the most expensive celebrity divorces ever, with Juanita receiving $168 million as part of the settlement.

Michael Jordan’s second wife is Yvette Prieto, whom he met in 2008 at a nightclub in Miami.

She is a Cuban-American model who has worked with designers like Alexander Wang.

They moved in together in 2009 and got engaged in 2011.

They got married on April 27, 2013, at the Episcopal Church of Bethesda-by-the-Sea in Palm Beach, Florida. The wedding was attended by about 500 guests, including celebrities like Tiger Woods, Spike Lee, Usher, and Robin Thicke.

Michael Jordan Kids

Jordan has five children from his two marriages: three sons and two daughters.

Jeffrey Michael Jordan is the oldest child of Michael and Juanita.

He was born on November 18, 1988.

He followed his father’s footsteps and played basketball at the University of Illinois and the University of Central Florida.

Marcus James Jordan is the second child of Michael and Juanita.

He was born on December 24, 1990.

Jasmine Mickael Jordan is the third child of Michael and Juanita.

She was born on December 7, 1992.

She graduated from Syracuse University with a degree in sports management.

Victoria Jordan and Ysabel Jordan are the twin daughters of Michael and Yvette.

They were born on February 9, 2014.

They are still very young and not much is known about them.

Michael Jordan is one of the richest athletes and celebrities in the world.

His net worth is estimated to be $2.6 billion as of September 2023, according to Forbes.

He earned most of his fortune from his basketball career, his endorsements, his investments, and his ownership of the Charlotte Hornets.

Michael Jordan Height: How Tall Is the NBA Legend?

Michael Jordan’s height is listed as 6 feet 6 inches (198 cm) by the NBA and other sources.

However, some people have speculated that he might be slightly shorter or taller than that based on various photos and videos of him standing next to other celebrities or athletes.

Michael Jordan stats

Michael Jordan’s stats are impressive and show why he is considered the greatest basketball player of all time. Here are some of his career highlights and achievements:

  • He played 15 seasons in the NBA, 13 with the Chicago Bulls and two with the Washington Wizards.
  • He averaged 30.1 points, 6.2 rebounds, 5.3 assists, and 2.3 steals per game in his career.
  • He won six NBA championships with the Bulls, all of them with Finals MVP honors.
  • He won five regular season MVP awards, 10 scoring titles, three steals titles, and one defensive player of the year award.
  • He was selected to 14 All-Star games, 11 All-NBA teams, and nine All-Defensive teams.
  • He was the Rookie of the Year in 1985 and the Slam Dunk Contest champion in 1987 and 1988.
  • He holds the NBA records for highest career scoring average, most points in a playoff game (63), most seasons leading the league in scoring (10), most consecutive games scoring at least 10 points (866), and most points scored in a single season (3,041).
  • He is the fourth all-time leading scorer in NBA history with 32,292 points, behind Kareem Abdul-Jabbar, Karl Malone, and LeBron James.
  • He is also a two-time Olympic gold medalist with Team USA in 1984 and 1992.

Conclusion

Michael Jordan is not only the greatest basketball player of all time but also a man of deep family connections and remarkable achievements.

Michael Jordan  parents, James R. Jordan Sr. and Deloris Peoples Jordan, played pivotal roles in shaping his character and work ethic, instilling values of hard work and integrity that carried him to greatness.

Jordan’s family expanded with five children from both marriages, each pursuing their own paths, whether in basketball or sports management.

As a basketball icon and cultural phenomenon, Michael Jordan’s legacy continues to inspire generations, making him not only a sports legend but a global symbol of excellence and determination.
